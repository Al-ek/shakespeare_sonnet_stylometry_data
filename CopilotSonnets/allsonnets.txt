CXCV

When first the hush of twilight veils the land,
Thy thoughts like lanterns glimmer in the shade;
They wander softly, guided by no hand,
Yet seeking still the paths thy dreams have made.
Why dost thou fear the quiet of the night,
When in its folds such gentle truths abide?
For even stars, though distant in their height,
Keep faithful watch o’er all the world they guide.
Let not thy doubts eclipse the rising morn,
Nor dim the promise waiting in the east;
For every soul, though weary, is reborn
When darkness yields and all its storms have ceased.
  Hold fast to hope, though shadows round thee lie,
  For dawn attends the brave who dare the sky.
CXCVI

The summer wind along the river strays,
And stirs the reeds with whispers soft and low;
So moves thy presence through my fleeting days,
A warmth that lingers everywhere I go.
Yet when thou’rt gone, the air grows still and cold,
As though the world forgot its gentle tune;
For joy, once bright, grows timid, faint, and old,
And silence deepens far too soon.
Return, sweet heart, and breathe upon the hours,
That time may bloom again with living grace;
Let absence yield to love’s reviving powers,
And sorrow fade before thy shining face.
  Come back, and let the waiting world be blessed,
  For all grows fair when thou art once possessed.
CXCVII

The sculptor Time with patient, steady art
Doth carve our fleeting moments into stone;
Yet though his chisel shapes the mortal heart,
The soul’s design remains forever known.
What form shall rise when all the years are done?
What figure shall emerge from joy and strife?
Shall it be one by noble purpose won,
Or one misshaped by fear’s uncertain knife?
Choose well, for though his strokes are firm and sure,
Thy will may guide the angle of his blade;
And though no mortal craft can long endure,
The spirit’s mark shall never wholly fade.
  So let thy courage shape what Time may hew,
  And thou shalt stand in form both strong and true.
CXCVIII

The winter moon upon the frozen field
Doth cast a path of pale, unbroken light;
Yet few there are who dare its truth revealed,
Or walk the lonely quiet of the night.
But thou, whose warmth defies the bitter air,
Dost tread where others tremble to begin;
For in thy breast there burns a flame so rare,
It melts the frost that gathers deep within.
Go forth, bold soul, and let thy fire ascend,
For even winter bows to hearts that blaze;
And though the winds their icy challenge send,
They cannot dim the fervor of thy days.
  Thy spark shall wake the sleeping earth once more,
  And spring shall bloom where winter ruled before.
CXCIX

When first I saw thee in the fading glow,
Thy form stood bright against the dying sun;
As though the day, reluctant yet to go,
Had left its final blessing on the run.
Since then, no dusk descends without thy name
Illuming all the shadows in its wake;
And though the night may swallow light and flame,
Thy memory keeps my trembling heart awake.
O let me hold that radiance ever near,
And guard it as the heavens guard their spheres;
For though the hours steal swiftly through the year,
Thy glow alone outshines my darkest fears.
  Shine on, and I shall never dread the night,
  For in thy presence all is turned to light.
CC

The restless sea, with ever-shifting face,
Reflects the moods that stir the human breast;
Now calm with peace, now wild in fierce embrace,
Now whispering dreams, now roaring unconfessed.
So too thy heart, though gentle at its core,
Doth move with tides no mortal chart may read;
Yet in its storms I find a truth the more,
For passion’s waves reveal our deepest need.
Let tempests rise, let winds their fury cast,
For love is not a harbor built for ease;
It stands the stronger when the storm is past,
And calmer still when soothed by tender breeze.
  Thus shall our hearts, though tossed by wind and sea,
  Find anchorage in shared fidelity.
CCI

The painter’s brush may catch a fleeting grace,
And bind in hue the beauty of an hour;
Yet never can he hold the living face,
Nor trap the breath that gives the blossom power.
So too my words, though shaped with earnest care,
Fall short of all thy presence would impart;
For language fails when wonder is too fair,
And silence speaks the fullness of the heart.
Yet still I write, compelled by love’s decree,
To chase the shadow of thy shining form;
Though knowing well no mortal verse can be
As bright as thou, whose smile alone keeps warm.
  Accept these lines, though pale beside thy light,
  For love alone gives strength enough to write.
CCII

The autumn leaves in robes of amber fall,
And drift like whispered secrets to the ground;
They heed no voice, no summons, none at all,
But follow where the quiet winds are bound.
So too our days, though cherished while they stay,
Slip softly from the branches of our years;
And though we grasp, they gently drift away,
Leaving behind their laughter and their tears.
Yet mourn them not, for beauty lies in change,
And endings sow the seeds of things to be;
The world renews itself through seasons strange,
And time unfolds its endless mystery.
  Embrace the fall, for though the leaves depart,
  Their colors linger long within the heart.
CCIII

The morning star that crowns the waking skies
Doth herald hope before the sun appears;
So shines thy truth before my doubting eyes,
A light that steadies all my trembling fears.
For though the night may press with heavy hand,
And cloud the path that once seemed clear and bright,
Thy constancy, like beacons on the land,
Restores my steps and guides me through the night.
Let storms arise, let darkness veil the way,
For still thy glow shall pierce the thickest gloom;
And though the world may falter in dismay,
Thy faith endures where lesser lights consume.
  Shine on, and I shall never lose my course,
  For hope in thee outlasts the tempest’s force.
CCIV

The tender bud that hides within the stem
Awaits the hour when warmth shall bid it rise;
It dreams of sunlit fields and jeweled hem,
And longs to greet the wide, unfolding skies.
So too thy heart, though cautious in its bloom,
Doth yearn for days when fear shall fade away;
It waits in silence, sheltered from the gloom,
For love to call it forth to brighter day.
Awake, dear soul, and let thy petals part,
For beauty thrives when courage leads the way;
And though the world may test the fragile heart,
Its strength grows sure when touched by gentle ray.
  Unfold at last, and trust the warming air,
  For all the spring was shaped to make thee fair.
CCV

The wandering breeze that stirs the forest deep
Doth carry tales from far, forgotten lands;
It wakes the boughs from their unbroken sleep,
And weaves its stories through their trembling hands.
So whispers fate upon the mortal ear,
A voice both strange and strangely understood;
It calls us forth from comfort into fear,
Yet leads us where our truest purpose stood.
Heed not the doubt that bids thee turn aside,
Nor cling to shores where thou hast long remained;
For life grows rich when hearts refuse to hide,
And seek the paths where destiny is gained.
  Go forth, and let the winds their counsel give,
  For only those who journey truly live.
CCVI

The quiet brook that winds through moss and stone
Doth murmur truths too soft for hurried minds;
It speaks to those who walk its banks alone,
And leave behind the noise the world designs.
So too thy thoughts, when stilled from daily care,
Reveal the depths that bustle would conceal;
For in the hush, the soul grows bright and fair,
And hidden wounds begin at last to heal.
Seek moments then where silence gently reigns,
And let thy spirit breathe its secret song;
For peace is found beyond the worldly chains,
And wisdom waits where quiet hours belong.
  Attend the brook, and thou shalt surely find
  The calm that shapes a clear and steadfast mind.
CCVII

The steadfast oak that stands against the storm
Doth not resist by strength of limb alone;
Its roots, unseen, in earth both deep and warm,
Hold firm the weight that winds would overthrow.
So too thy soul, though tested by the years,
Finds anchorage in truths that lie below;
Not in the fleeting triumphs or the fears,
But in the quiet virtues none may show.
Stand firm, dear heart, when tempests round thee rise,
And trust the ground that shaped thee from the start;
For though the clouds may darken all the skies,
Thy roots shall hold through every wrench and smart.
  Endure, and thou shalt see the storm depart,
  And sunlight fall again upon thy heart.
CCVIII

The distant bell that rings at break of day
Doth summon all to rise from slumber deep;
It calls the weary forth upon their way,
And bids the world awaken from its sleep.
So rings the call of purpose in thy breast,
A tone that stirs the marrow of thy will;
It urges thee to leave thy place of rest,
And climb the heights where dreams are waiting still.
Delay no more, for time is swift and stern,
And hours lost return to none again;
Let not regret be harvest for thy turn,
Nor fear the cost of striving among men.
  Rise now, and heed the summons of thy soul,
  For only those who answer shall be whole.
CCIX

The lantern’s glow that flickers in the rain
Doth cast a trembling circle on the ground;
Yet though the storm may lash with cold disdain,
Its fragile flame refuses to be drowned.
So too thy hope, though battered by despair,
Holds fast against the winds that round thee roar;
For in its light, though faint, there lingers care,
A promise that the night shall be no more.
Guard well that spark, and shield it from the gale,
For even embers kindle mighty fires;
And though the world may whisper thou wilt fail,
Thy flame shall rise to meet thy heart’s desires.
  Let hope burn on, though tempests round thee sweep,
  For light survives where faith is rooted deep.
CCX

The poet’s quill, though humble in its frame,
Doth wield a power greater than the sword;
It shapes the world through memory and flame,
And binds the ages with a single word.
So too thy voice, though quiet in its tone,
May stir the hearts of those who pause to hear;
For truth, once spoken, stands not alone,
But echoes on through every listening ear.
Speak then with grace, and let thy meaning rise,
Unfettered by the doubts that silence breeds;
For even whispers reach the distant skies,
And gentle words may plant the strongest seeds.
  Lift up thy voice, and let thy spirit sing,
  For songs of truth outlast the passing spring.
CCXI

The mountain path that winds through mist and stone
Doth challenge those who seek its distant crest;
Yet though the climb be steep and oft unknown,
The view above rewards the earnest quest.
So too the road that leads to wisdom’s height
Demands of thee both patience and resolve;
For none may reach the summit by mere might,
But only those whose hearts refuse to dissolve.
Press on, though clouds obscure the rising peak,
And trust the steps that brought thee to this place;
For every trial strengthens what is weak,
And every fall prepares a firmer pace.
  Ascend, and thou shalt see the world anew,
  For heights reveal what valleys never knew.
CCXII

The evening breeze that cools the weary land
Doth soothe the fields where heat had held its reign;
It brings relief with gentle, healing hand,
And lifts the weight of long-endured pain.
So too thy kindness, soft as twilight air,
Doth ease the burdens others cannot bear;
It moves unseen, yet leaves a comfort rare,
A balm that lingers long when thou art there.
Let not the world, in haste or harsh design,
Diminish what thy tender heart bestows;
For though thy gifts may seem too small to shine,
They bloom in souls like ever-living rose.
  Continue thus, and thou shalt surely see
  How gentle deeds reshape humanity.
CCXIII

The restless flame that dances in the hearth
Doth warm the room with ever-changing light;
It leaps with joy, then settles into mirth,
A living spirit flickering through the night.
So too thy passion, bright with shifting hue,
Doth kindle life in all it touches near;
It burns with dreams both old and ever new,
And drives away the creeping shade of fear.
Yet guard it well, lest zeal consume its core,
For fire unchecked may scorch the tender ground;
But tended with a steady hand once more,
It glows with strength serene and unbound.
  Let passion burn, but let thy wisdom guide,
  For flame and thought together shall abide.
CCXIV

The final star that lingers in the morn
Doth fade with grace as daylight claims the sky;
Yet though its glow be lost when day is born,
Its memory shines where waking spirits lie.
So too the moments cherished in the past
Dissolve like dew beneath the rising sun;
Yet though they fade, their sweetness still shall last,
And shape the heart long after they are done.
Cling not to what the dawn must take away,
Nor mourn the hours that cannot be restored;
For life renews itself with every day,
And gifts anew what time before had stored.
  Embrace the light, and let the old depart,
  For dawn brings peace to every seeking heart.
