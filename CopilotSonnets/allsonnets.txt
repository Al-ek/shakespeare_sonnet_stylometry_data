I  
When dawn first breaks and spills her golden fire,
My thoughts ascend to realms where you abide;
For in your light my weary hopes aspire,
And all my doubts fall silent at your side.
The world may turn with tempests yet untamed,
And fortune’s wheel may toss me where it will,
But in your gaze my trembling heart is framed,
And every restless pulse is rendered still.
Let time advance with ever‑changing face,
Let seasons fade and steal their colors too;
No force can shift my soul from its true place,
For all my wandering winds return to you.
So long as breath within my breast remains,
Your name shall echo through my sweetest strains.

II  
The night, though dark, reveals a deeper glow
When stars in secret counsel softly gleam;
So too your virtues, hidden from the show,
Shine brightest when the world forgets to dream.
Men praise the sun for all its open grace,
Yet fail to see the moon’s more tender art;
But I, who trace the shadows of your face,
Find gentler radiance dwelling in your heart.
Let others chase the loud applause of day,
And seek their glory in the public eye;
I cherish quiet truths that softly stay,
Like whispered vows no storm can ever buy.
Thus in the hush where silent wonders start,
Your light alone commands my faithful heart.

III  
O fickle hour that steals my joy away,
Why must you hasten when I hold delight?
Your wings are swifter in the fairest day,
Yet drag like lead in sorrow’s lingering night.
If I could bind you with a lover’s chain,
Or bribe you with the treasures of my soul,
I’d keep you near and never part again,
And make your fleeting minutes fully whole.
But time obeys no mortal’s fond desire,
Nor bends its march to suit a lover’s plea;
It burns our hopes with unrelenting fire,
And leaves but ash where dreams once longed to be.
Yet though you fly, I’ll greet you without fear,
For every loss makes each new hour more dear.

IV  
Your voice, a breeze that stirs the sleeping rose,
Awakes the petals of my guarded mind;
In every word a secret garden grows,
Where peace and wonder blossom intertwined.
Though storms may rage beyond our sheltered bower,
And thunder shake the trembling fields of men,
Your gentle tone restores my fading power,
And bids my spirit rise to strength again.
No music wrought by mortal hand or art
Could match the cadence of your living song;
It weaves its echoes through my yearning heart,
And rights the wrongs that time has rendered wrong.
So speak once more, and let the world be still,
For in your breath I find my sovereign will.

V  
The mirror lies when gazing on your grace,
For glass can hold but shadows of the true;
It captures form yet misses all the place
Where virtue dwells and paints the soul anew.
Your beauty lives not merely in your frame,
Nor in the fleeting bloom of mortal hue;
It rises from a nobler, brighter flame
That warms the hearts of all who look on you.
Let others trust the surface of the eye,
And judge by lines that time will soon erase;
I read the script no years can nullify,
The inner light no winter chill can chase.
Thus though the mirror shows what time may take,
Your deeper splendor none shall ever break.

VI  
When envy stirs the hearts of lesser men,
And whispers poison in their eager ears,
I stand unmoved, for I remember when
Your steadfast truth dispelled my darkest fears.
The world may cast its judgments far and wide,
And weigh your worth on scales of shifting sand;
But I, who walked in sorrow at your side,
Know well the strength that guides your gentle hand.
Let slander strike with tongues of sharpened spite,
And fashion storms from clouds of empty air;
Your constancy shall turn their day to night,
And prove their malice powerless and bare.
For though the world may falter in its view,
My faith stands firm, and ever rests in you.

VII  
The harvest moon ascends her silver throne,
And bathes the fields in soft, enchanted light;
So does your presence make my spirit known,
And lift my weary thoughts to nobler height.
Where once I wandered lost in barren lands,
With hope a stranger to my troubled breast,
You took my trembling soul into your hands,
And gave my restless heart a place to rest.
No earthly crown nor gem of royal store
Could match the gift your quiet kindness brings;
It opens doors no mortal locked before,
And frees my voice to rise on fearless wings.
Thus as the moon commands the dreaming sea,
Your grace commands the better part of me.

VIII  
I sought for wisdom in the halls of age,
Where scholars keep their tomes of ancient lore;
But found no truth within a dusty page
That matched the lessons you reveal far more.
For in your gaze I read a deeper art,
A knowledge born of love’s enduring flame;
It speaks in silence to my listening heart,
And writes its counsel where no years can claim.
Let sages boast of all they understand,
And chart the stars with learned, lofty pride;
Your simple touch outshines their guiding hand,
And leads me where their maps cannot provide.
Thus though the world may praise the wise and strong,
Your quiet truth has been my guide all along.

IX  
The winter wind may howl with bitter breath,
And strip the branches of their summer cheer;
Yet even in the face of seeming death,
Your warmth remains and keeps my spirit near.
For though the frost may bind the silent earth,
And cloak the fields in robes of icy white,
Your kindness sparks a gentler, brighter birth,
And turns the coldest hour into light.
Let tempests rage and shake the trembling sky,
And drive the timid heart to seek retreat;
Your steadfast love shall teach me not to fly,
But stand with courage firm upon my feet.
Thus winter’s chill, though fierce it seems to be,
Grows mild when I recall your constancy.

X  
O wandering thought that drifts from place to place,
Why do you flee when I would hold you near?
You chase the memory of a vanished face,
And stir the embers of a long‑lost year.
Yet though you roam through shadows of the past,
And haunt the corners of my waking mind,
I know your restless journey cannot last,
For time will tame the ghosts you leave behind.
Let sorrow speak its piece and then depart,
And leave no lingering echo in its wake;
For though it weighs upon my tender heart,
It teaches strength no joy could ever make.
Thus wandering thought, return when you are free,
And bring the peace that once belonged to me.

XI  
Your laughter, like a fountain in the spring,
Revives the barren soil of my despair;
It bids the frozen earth to bloom and sing,
And fills the silent air with colors rare.
No jewel forged in fire or mined in stone
Could match the brightness of your living cheer;
It lifts the heaviest sorrow I have known,
And turns my darkest hour to crystal clear.
Let others chase their pleasures far and wide,
And seek delight in fleeting, worldly things;
Your joy alone can shift the inward tide,
And grant my weary soul its needed wings.
So laugh once more, and let the heavens hear,
For in your mirth my heart grows bold and clear.

XII  
The river bends though mountains bar its way,
And carves its path with patient, steady grace;
So too your will, though trials darken day,
Moves ever forward to its destined place.
No force of fate, nor weight of mortal care,
Can halt the quiet strength within your breast;
It rises firm against the world’s despair,
And finds its triumph where the brave find rest.
Let others falter when the road grows steep,
And yield their hopes to shadows of the night;
Your courage wakes the lion from its sleep,
And bids it roar with unrelenting might.
Thus though the world may doubt what you can do,
I trust the path that bends but never breaks in you.

XIII  
The painter’s hand may craft a lovely scene,
With colors bright and shapes of perfect form;
Yet none can match the beauty I have seen
When you stand calm amid the rising storm.
For in your poise a deeper art resides,
A grace no mortal brush could e’er contain;
It holds its ground though shifting fortune chides,
And stands unshaken in the face of pain.
Let artists chase the fleeting light of day,
And capture moments destined soon to fade;
Your steadfast soul outshines their grand display,
And casts a glow no shadow ever made.
Thus though the world may praise their painted view,
The truest masterpiece I find in you.

XIV  
When silence falls and dims the noisy crowd,
I hear the echo of your distant thought;
It speaks more clearly than the voices loud
That fill the world with words of little aught.
Your quiet mind, though gentle in its tone,
Commands a power deeper than the strong;
It shapes the truth that others leave unknown,
And rights the errors time has rendered wrong.
Let boastful tongues proclaim their hollow might,
And drown the air with thunderous decree;
Your silent wisdom shines with purer light,
And guides my wandering heart to clarity.
Thus in the hush where lesser voices cease,
Your whispered truth becomes my perfect peace.

XV  
The rose that blooms beneath the summer sun
May boast its beauty for a fleeting hour;
But when the day and all its warmth are done,
Its petals fall and lose their fragile power.
Yet though such blossoms fade without a trace,
Your virtues stand untouched by time’s decay;
They hold their bloom in every changing place,
And brighten even sorrow’s darkest day.
Let nature’s gifts be praised for what they seem,
And cherished while their colors still remain;
Your worth endures beyond the sweetest dream,
And grows more radiant through every pain.
Thus though the rose may wilt when seasons part,
Your lasting beauty lives within my heart.

XVI  
O restless sea that churns with ceaseless might,
Why do you rage when calm would better serve?
Your waves, though wild, reflect my inner fight,
And mirror all the storms I can’t preserve.
Yet in your depths I see a hidden peace,
A quiet realm untouched by wind or tide;
It whispers softly that my fears may cease,
And bids my troubled thoughts to gently glide.
Let tempests roar upon your foaming crest,
And shake the fragile vessels of the brave;
Your secret stillness in your silent breast
Reminds me strength lies deeper than the wave.
Thus though I face the storms that life may send,
Your quiet truth shall guide me to my end.

XVII  
Your memory walks beside me through the day,
A faithful shadow cast by love’s bright flame;
It follows close though miles may stretch the way,
And speaks your presence though I call no name.
No distance wide, nor time’s relentless pace,
Can dim the imprint of your gentle care;
It lingers soft in every empty space,
And fills the silence with the thought of where
You once had stood, and where you stand no more,
Yet still remain in all I hope to be;
For though you dwell upon a distant shore,
Your spirit moves in quiet grace through me.
Thus memory, though bittersweet and true,
Becomes the bridge that keeps me close to you.

XVIII  
The morning dew upon the waking field
Reflects the promise of a newborn day;
So does your kindness, tenderly revealed,
Restore my hope in life’s uncertain way.
For though the world may bruise the gentle heart,
And burden souls with trials unforeseen,
Your mercy plays a soft, redeeming part,
And turns the harshest winter into green.
Let others guard their love with iron walls,
And fear the wounds that open trust may bring;
Your open hand, though vulnerable, still calls
The weary spirit forth to rise and sing.
Thus in your grace I find a brighter view,
And learn the strength that only kindness knew.

XIX  
The poet seeks for words that might convey
The depth of feeling hidden in his breast;
Yet language fails, and all his lines betray
The truth that cannot fully be expressed.
So too I struggle when I speak of you,
For every phrase falls short of what I feel;
No metaphor nor image can renew
The living warmth your presence makes so real.
Let others craft their verses rich and grand,
And weave their rhymes with artful, measured care;
My simple truth no flourish could command,
Yet speaks more plainly than their finest air.
Thus though my words may falter as they rise,
My heart still knows the truth your love supplies.

XX  
O final thought that crowns my weary mind,
You come at dusk when all the world grows still;
You gather up the dreams I left behind,
And shape them gently to your quiet will.
No fear remains when you at last appear,
For in your calm my troubled doubts take flight;
You soothe the wounds that linger from the year,
And guide my spirit through the tender night.
Let morning bring its labors as it must,
And fill my hours with duties yet undone;
Your evening grace restores my deepest trust,
And tells me peace is never truly gone.
Thus ends my verse, yet not my heart’s refrain,
For thoughts of you shall evermore remain.
