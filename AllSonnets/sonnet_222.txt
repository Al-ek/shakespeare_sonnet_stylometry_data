CCXXII

As rivers that have long been held in drought
Do rush with double fury when the rain
At last descends and breaks the dry spell out,
And flood the cracked and thirsty plain again:
So I, long parched by sorrow's governance,
Did meet thy kindness like a sudden flood,
And felt the whole arrested life advance
And quicken through the channels of my blood.
Too long the fields of me had known the heat
Of longing unassuaged by any hand,
Too long had I made famine into meat
And learnt to count as full what was but sand.
    Then came thy rain, and all the plains of me
    Grew green with more than I had hoped to see.
