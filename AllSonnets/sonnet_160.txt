CLX

When I consider how the minutes fly,
Like restless birds across a stormy sea,
I fear the time when beauty's rose must die,
And leave me nothing but the thought of thee.
But as the seed within the earth doth wait,
To spring to life when April's breath is near,
So shall thy glory find a second state,
And in a thousand different forms appear.
For thou art part of all that's good and fair,
The light of stars, the sweetness of the spring,
And while there's music in the summer air,
Thy name shall be the song the angels sing.
Then fear not time, nor death's eternal night,
For thou art made of everlasting light.
