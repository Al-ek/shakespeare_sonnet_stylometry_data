CLXXXIX

The night, though clad in sable, is not void,
But sown with stars that mock her seeming grief;
So in thy darkest hours are joys deployed,
Small lights that yield the spirit sweet relief.
Look up when earthly prospects fade from view,
And thou shalt see what daylight hid from sight;
For heaven reserves her clearer truths for few
Who dare to study constellations bright.
Despair is but a cloud that dims the scope,
Not an eclipse ordained to last for aye;
Behind its veil stands undiminished hope,
Prepared to chase the stubborn gloom away.
Trust that no shadow rules with endless sway,
For every night prepares a dawning day.
