CLXXVIII

The glass that shows thee what thou art today
Doth flatter less than memory of youth;
It whispers softly how thy hairs turn gray,
And trades bright certainty for tempered truth.
Yet in those lines where careful years have writ
Their chronicles upon thy patient brow,
There dwells a wisdom none can counterfeit,
A deeper charm than summer doth allow.
Despise not age, that sober counsellor,
Whose furrowed hand bestows a steadier sight;
For painted dawn is but a promise poor
Compared with noon in full meridian light.
So let thy fading serve a fairer end:
In ripeness lives the harvest time doth send.
