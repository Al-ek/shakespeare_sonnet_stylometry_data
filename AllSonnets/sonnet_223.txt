CCXXIII

What profit hath the astronomer of stars,
Who maps the patient courses of the sky
And reckons up the influences of Mars
Yet cannot say for whom those planets fly?
His tables tell him where the wanderers go
And what conjunctions govern what affairs,
But love observes no table that we know
And pays no heed to how the heavens square.
I would not have the stars dictate my heart
Nor place my choosing in the zodiac's hand,
For love is not so mathematical an art
As charts and spheres could ever understand:
    The stars may tell thee what the seasons bring,
    But only thou canst tell me everything.
