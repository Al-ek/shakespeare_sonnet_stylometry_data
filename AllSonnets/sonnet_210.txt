CCX

The poet’s quill, though humble in its frame,
Doth wield a power greater than the sword;
It shapes the world through memory and flame,
And binds the ages with a single word.
So too thy voice, though quiet in its tone,
May stir the hearts of those who pause to hear;
For truth, once spoken, stands not alone,
But echoes on through every listening ear.
Speak then with grace, and let thy meaning rise,
Unfettered by the doubts that silence breeds;
For even whispers reach the distant skies,
And gentle words may plant the strongest seeds.
  Lift up thy voice, and let thy spirit sing,
  For songs of truth outlast the passing spring.
