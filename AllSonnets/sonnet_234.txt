CCXXXIV

Now let me close these verses as one shuts
A window on the evening, not in haste,
But with the care of one who never cuts
The end before the full moment is traced.
For I have loved thee in these numbered lines
As fully as my given art allows,
And if some stanza stumbles or repines,
Let effort be the witness of my vows.
The sonnets of the great lie long before
These humble efforts, casting lengths of shade,
Yet even in that shadow I adore
The chance to honour what thy presence made:
    Let greater poets speak of grander things —
    I'll spend my lesser gift on what love brings.
