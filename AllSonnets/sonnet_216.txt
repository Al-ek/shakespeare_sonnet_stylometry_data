CCXVI

What tongue hath ever shaped the proper word
For that which steals upon the sleeping heart,
As dawn arrives unbidden and unstirred,
And lights the chamber ere the sleeper start?
So came thy face upon my waking mind
Before I knew the name of what I saw,
A visitation of the sweetest kind
That left my former reason full of awe.
For love doth not announce itself with drums
Nor send before it any herald bright,
But like the tide without a whisper comes
And finds the shore already in its might.
    So I was taken ere I could defend,
    And gladly lost, and glad to stay thus end.
