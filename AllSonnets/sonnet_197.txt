CXCVII

The sculptor Time with patient, steady art
Doth carve our fleeting moments into stone;
Yet though his chisel shapes the mortal heart,
The soul’s design remains forever known.
What form shall rise when all the years are done?
What figure shall emerge from joy and strife?
Shall it be one by noble purpose won,
Or one misshaped by fear’s uncertain knife?
Choose well, for though his strokes are firm and sure,
Thy will may guide the angle of his blade;
And though no mortal craft can long endure,
The spirit’s mark shall never wholly fade.
  So let thy courage shape what Time may hew,
  And thou shalt stand in form both strong and true.
