CLXXXVII

Not steel nor stone the firmest bulwark prove,
When once corruption saps their inward might;
So vows unwatered by continual love
Grow brittle in the weathering of spite.
Renew thy promises with daily care,
As gardeners tend the vine with watchful eye;
For weeds of doubt spring rank in neglect there,
And choke the fruit that else would multiply.
Let kindness be the sunshine of thy speech,
And patience like a soft, unceasing rain;
So shall affection ripen out of reach
Of petty storms that breed from pride or pain.
In constant nurture let thy troth abide,
And love shall stand though lesser passions slide.
