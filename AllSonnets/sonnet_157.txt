CLVII

Why should I labor with a weary pen,
To paint the wonders of thy outward show?
Thou art the marvel of the eyes of men,
A flower that in the garden of the gods doth grow.
But inward worth is more than outward fair,
And truth is richer than the finest gold;
Thy gentle soul is like the summer air,
Which keeps a sweetness that can ne'er be told.
Then let the poets of a future age,
Look on these lines and find thy image there,
For thou shalt live upon the living page,
As long as men shall breathe and find thee fair.
Thy name shall be a lamp to light the way,
When all our monuments are turned to clay.
