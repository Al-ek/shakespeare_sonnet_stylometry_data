CLXXIX

If envy gnaw upon thy neighbor’s fame,
And bid thee measure worth by others’ gain,
Consider how the self-consuming flame
Devours the heart wherein it would remain.
The laurel thrives not by another’s fall,
Nor doth the eagle mount by clipping wings;
Each star maintains his station in the hall,
And from his proper sphere due luster brings.
Content thee with the orbit heaven gave,
And strive therein thy brightness to refine;
For he that would another’s glory have
Makes shipwreck of the compass of his own.
In thine own course let excellence be shown,
And thou shalt reign secure upon thy throne.
