CCXXVIII

The painter that would capture morning light
Must work with patience ere the moment flies,
For dawn will not be still for mortal sight
And gold turns grey before the beholder's eyes.
How much the greater task is mine therefore,
Who seek to fix not light but living grace,
To hold in words what words were not made for
And set thy spirit in a staying place.
My colours are but letters, black and thin,
My canvas but the fragile page of time,
Yet something of thee enters in between
The syllables of every earnest rhyme:
    What painters lose to light, I hope to keep —
    Thy truest likeness, not in paint, but deep.
