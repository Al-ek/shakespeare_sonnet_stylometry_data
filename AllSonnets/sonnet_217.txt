CCXVII

The sundial marks the hours that it may,
But clouds may come and steal the gnomon's art,
And so the measure of my longest day
Is reckoned not by sun but by thy heart.
When thou art present, time doth hold its tongue
And stands apart as one who dares not speak,
But absence is a bell forever rung
That fills each hour and hollows every week.
O that the dial of the world could freeze
Upon one golden afternoon in June,
When thou didst laugh and put my mind at ease
And summer hung contented with the moon:
    But clocks are blind to what a lover knows,
    And time moves on wherever sweetness goes.
