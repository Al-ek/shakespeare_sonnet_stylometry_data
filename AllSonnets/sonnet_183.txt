CLXXXIII

Thou art a music wrought of varied strings,
Where mirth and melancholy intertwine;
Now joy in nimble, golden cadence springs,
Now sighing notes in minor fall and pine.
To choose but one were discord to the whole,
For harmony from contraries is born;
The shadow lends a contour to the soul,
And night prepares the splendor of the morn.
Despise not then the sadness in thy breast,
Nor chase it hence as though it were a foe;
It is the depth whereby thy joys are blessed,
The darker soil where richer roses grow.
Accept each mood as part of nature’s art,
And find full symphony within thy heart.
