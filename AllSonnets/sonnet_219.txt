CCXIX

My glass hath made me privy to the truth
That years do levy on a willing face,
How time doth slowly audit all the youth
And leaves behind a more considerate grace.
Yet what the mirror shows I do not fear,
For thou hast taught me that the body's sum
Is less than what the spirit holds most dear,
The depth below the skin that lines become.
Then let the temples silver, and the brow
Record in careful script the years it bore,
I would not barter what I carry now
For all the untried smoothness gone before:
    For I have loved and been by loving schooled,
    And by that learning, wisely been refueled.
