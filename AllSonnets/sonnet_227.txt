CCXXVII

Let those who prize the lustre of a name
Build high their reputations in the crowd,
And court the warm periphery of fame
And wrap themselves in its uncertain shroud.
For fame is but the shadow of a thought
That multitudes have shared for half a breath,
A fragile currency too dearly bought
By those who've traded living close to death.
I have no use for that which crowds bestow
And withdraw again before the sun goes down,
For I have something crowds can never know,
A private and a more enduring crown:
    Thy love is what I'd wear against the tide,
    Worth all the fame that ever fame supplied.
