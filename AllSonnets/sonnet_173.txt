CLXXIII

When forty winters have consumed my blood,
And left me like a tree in winter's frost,
I'll think of how thy beauty was a flood,
That saved my spirit when it was most lost.
The world may forget the songs that I have sung,
And all the labors of my weary hand,
But as long as there's a voice and human tongue,
Thy name shall echo through a happy land.
For thou art made of that immortal stuff,
That neither time nor fortune can decay,
And though the road of life be dark and rough,
Thy love shall be the lamp to light my way.
Then let me close my eyes in quiet sleep,
For I have found a treasure I can keep.
