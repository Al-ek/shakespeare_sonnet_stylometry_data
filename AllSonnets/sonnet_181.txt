CLXXXI

When sorrow like a winter storm descends
And strips the branches of their summer cheer,
The heart, unmantled, to the tempest bends
And thinks no kindly April shall appear.
Yet roots that drink beneath the frozen ground
Prepare unseen the miracle of spring;
Though frost imprison all the fields around,
Within the core new-quickened pulses sing.
So grief, though harsh in its dominion brief,
May tutor hope in patience and in might;
For out of long endurance of our grief
Breaks forth a more resilient kind of light.
Then faint not thou beneath affliction’s rod,
For storms are but the husbandmen of God.
