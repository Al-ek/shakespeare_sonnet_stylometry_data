CCXV

When winter's breath hath stripped the elm tree bare,
And frost doth seal the murmuring brook in glass,
What comfort then remains for those who wear
The heavy cloak of time that none may pass?
Yet in the marrow of the coldest night
Some ember keeps its counsel, red and low,
As love, once kindled, holds its threadbare light
Against the dark that all things come to know.
So thou, whose warmth did furnish all my days,
Art not diminished though the season turn,
For in the record of my grateful phrase
Thy name shall find a hearth wherein to burn:
    The frost may take the flower from the bough,
    But cannot frost the verse that keeps thee now.
