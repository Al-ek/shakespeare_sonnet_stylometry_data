CCII

The autumn leaves in robes of amber fall,
And drift like whispered secrets to the ground;
They heed no voice, no summons, none at all,
But follow where the quiet winds are bound.
So too our days, though cherished while they stay,
Slip softly from the branches of our years;
And though we grasp, they gently drift away,
Leaving behind their laughter and their tears.
Yet mourn them not, for beauty lies in change,
And endings sow the seeds of things to be;
The world renews itself through seasons strange,
And time unfolds its endless mystery.
  Embrace the fall, for though the leaves depart,
  Their colors linger long within the heart.
