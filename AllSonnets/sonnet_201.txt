CCI

The painter’s brush may catch a fleeting grace,
And bind in hue the beauty of an hour;
Yet never can he hold the living face,
Nor trap the breath that gives the blossom power.
So too my words, though shaped with earnest care,
Fall short of all thy presence would impart;
For language fails when wonder is too fair,
And silence speaks the fullness of the heart.
Yet still I write, compelled by love’s decree,
To chase the shadow of thy shining form;
Though knowing well no mortal verse can be
As bright as thou, whose smile alone keeps warm.
  Accept these lines, though pale beside thy light,
  For love alone gives strength enough to write.
