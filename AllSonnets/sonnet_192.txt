CXCII

The eye enamored of exterior show
May miss the jewel hid in modest dress;
For glittering surfaces too often throw
A gaudy veil on inward emptiness.
Seek thou the substance under painted guise,
And test with trial what first sight commends;
For truth withstands the scrutiny of eyes,
While falsehood at a searching glance soon bends.
So in thy friendships weigh not wealth nor face,
But probe the temper of the chosen heart;
There lies the measure of enduring grace,
Not in the ornaments of borrowed art.
Prize what is sound beneath the outward frame,
And thou shalt seldom entertain a shame.
