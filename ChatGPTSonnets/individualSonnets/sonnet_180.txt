CLXXX

The tide of favor ebbs as swift it flows,
And fickle crowds their borrowed idols break;
Today they crown with garlands and with oaths,
Tomorrow’s breath the selfsame tongues forsake.
Trust not the trumpet’s loud but hollow cry,
Nor build thy dwelling on applause of men;
For windy praise doth into silence die,
And leaves thee poorer than thou wert ere then.
But if thy conscience stand a faithful guard,
And inward truth approve what thou hast done,
No outward tempest strikes thy peace so hard
As to eclipse the witness of the sun.
Seek thou that court where judgment is sincere,
And scorn the market of the vulgar cheer.
