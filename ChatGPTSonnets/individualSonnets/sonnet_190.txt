CXC

The breath of rumor, wandering and wild,
Makes monstrous shapes of trifles lightly born;
A careless word by malice reconciled
Returns in armor sharper than a thorn.
Guard well thy tongue, yet more thy listening ear,
That thou admit no falsehood to thy trust;
For slander thrives where men incline to fear
And feed suspicion with unwholesome gust.
Examine first, and judge with tempered mind,
Lest thou become the herald of a lie;
For once set free, no rein can rumor bind,
Nor call her back though truth protest and cry.
Let charity before thy verdict stand,
And mercy guide the sentence of thy hand.
