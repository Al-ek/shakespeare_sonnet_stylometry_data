CLXXXV

The face thou show’st the world is but a veil,
A courteous mask for market and for street;
Behind it dwell desires that oft assail
The ordered calm that strangers think complete.
O let there be one chamber of thy breast
Wherein no counterfeit need find employ,
But truth, unarmed, may harbor and may rest
Secure from censure, rivalry, or noise.
For he that lives divided from his core
Doth waste his strength in endless self-defense;
While simple candor, fearing none before,
Stands like a tower of quiet confidence.
Be thou at one within thy secret heart,
And outward shows shall need no studied art.
