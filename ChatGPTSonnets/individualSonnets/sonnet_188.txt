CLXXXVIII

The child thou wert still lingers in thine eyes,
Though manhood’s beard proclaim advancing years;
In sudden laughter innocence defies
The careful discipline of prudent fears.
Cherish that spark which time would fain suppress,
Lest custom’s yoke too heavily descend;
For wonder is the fountain of excess
From which imagination draws her friend.
Without it life grows mechanical and dry,
A ledger kept with uninspired hand;
But with it even common things may vie
With marvels scattered through a fabled land.
Preserve within thy breast that youthful flame,
And age shall find thee vigorous the same.
