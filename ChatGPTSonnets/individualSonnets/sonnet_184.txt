CLXXXIV

Ambition, winged with fire, would scale the height
Where fame upon a narrow summit stands;
Yet thin the air that feeds such perilous flight,
And weak the foothold of aspiring hands.
How many, dazzled by the distant gleam,
Have lost the safer path beneath their feet;
Pursuing still a half-imagined dream,
They stumble where success and ruin meet.
But he that climbs with measured, patient pace,
Esteeming more the journey than the prize,
Finds in each step a tempering of grace
And learns true greatness under humbler skies.
Aspire, yet let discretion be thy guide,
Lest mounting high, thou fall from wounded pride.
