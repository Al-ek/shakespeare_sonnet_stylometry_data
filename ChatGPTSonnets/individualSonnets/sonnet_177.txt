CLXXVII

What profit lies in solitary praise,
When echo only answers to thy song?
The sweetest lute, untouched for many days,
Grows harsh with silence, having slept too long.
So is thy heart, if barred from kind exchange,
A garden walled against the liberal spring;
Though fertile still, its blossoms suffer change,
Denied the commerce gentle seasons bring.
Unclose thy gates, and let affection sow
Those seeds that else in barren soil shall die;
For love that in another’s breast doth grow
Returns with harvest multiplied thereby.
Give forth thyself, and thou shalt richer be,
In losing self, thou gain’st eternity.
