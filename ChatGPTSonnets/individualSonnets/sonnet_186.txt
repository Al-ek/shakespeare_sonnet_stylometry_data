CLXXXVI

The hours I spend in absence from thy sight
Move slow as shadows on a winter’s wall;
Each minute limps, bereft of its delight,
And heedless clocks in sullen cadence call.
Yet when thy presence blesses once again
The common air wherein we breathe and speak,
Time, erst so harsh, forgets his former pain
And grows prodigal of the joy we seek.
Thus art thou sovereign of my measured days,
Commanding swiftness or reluctant stay;
In thy regard lies hidden all the ways
By which my seasons flourish or decay.
O grant me but the tenure of thy glance,
And I shall thrive in love’s sweet governance.
