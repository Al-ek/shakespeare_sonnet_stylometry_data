CXCI

The sea of fortune alters with the moon,
Now lifting high, now leaving vessels bare;
He laughs at morning who by afternoon
May sit companioned only by despair.
Yet wisdom trims her sails to either tide,
Nor boasts in plenty nor in want repines;
She makes of adverse winds a lawful guide
And reads advantage in untoward signs.
So shalt thou steer amid uncertain chance,
If moderation hold the helm secure;
For extremes invite a perilous advance,
While steady courses longest shall endure.
In even temper let thy spirit be,
And thou shalt master fate’s inconstancy.
