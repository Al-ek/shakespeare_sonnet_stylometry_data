CXCIV

At last when age shall close thy mortal eyes
And bid thee render up thy borrowed breath,
What treasure shall accompany thee rise
To plead thy cause against the bar of death?
Not titles, lands, nor flattering renown,
For these dissolve as frost before the sun;
But conscience clear shall be thy lasting crown,
And faithful love the record thou hast won.
Live then so well that when the summons comes,
Thou need’st not quake before the final sea;
Let virtue be the music of thy drums,
And hope the pilot of eternity.
So shalt thou pass, yet not to darkness driven,
For righteous lives make luminous their heaven.
