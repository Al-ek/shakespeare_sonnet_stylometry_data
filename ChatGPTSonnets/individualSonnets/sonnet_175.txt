CLXXV

Why dost thou hide thy light in jealous shade,
As though the sun were debtor to the night?
What fear persuades thee beauty’s coin to fade,
And lock its treasure from the public sight?
The rose, though brief her sovereignty of hours,
Unfolds her wealth to every passing eye;
She hoards not fragrance in her guarded bowers,
Nor bids the wandering summer breeze deny.
If thou, self-prisoned in thy silent grace,
Dost keep thy glory sealed from common air,
Time’s subtle thief shall rifle every trace
And leave thee poorer than thou wert most fair.
Spend, then, thy riches ere they waste away,
For unused gold doth tarnish into clay.
