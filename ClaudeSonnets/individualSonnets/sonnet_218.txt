CCXVIII

Shall I compare the tenure of thy grace
To monuments that proud antiquity
Hath raised in stone to fix some sovereign face
Against the long assault of memory?
The stone grows porous where the acid rains
Descend through centuries of patient toil,
And marble yields at last to what remains
When all the sculptor's confidence doth spoil.
But thou art not of stone, nor wouldst thou wish
The cold immortal prison that it gives,
For living breath is sweeter than this dish
Of deathless dust that neither breathes nor lives.
    Let stone praise stone — I'll have thee warm and brief,
    And mourn thy passing with a mortal grief.
