CCXXIX

Why doth the rose decline within the week
That should by right be given many months?
And why doth beauty in the fairest cheek
Concede too soon to time's impatient stunts?
Nature, it seems, hath learned a cruel art
Of hoarding time from what deserves the most,
And giving freely to the dullest part
What finer things have never come to boast.
Yet in this very brevity there lies
A lesson that the permanent doth miss,
For what endures grows common to the eyes
While rarity lends sweetness to the kiss:
    Thou art more precious for the time thou'lt spend,
    And dearer for the knowing of thine end.
