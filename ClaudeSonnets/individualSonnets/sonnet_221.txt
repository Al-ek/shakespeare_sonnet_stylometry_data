CCXXI

O thou that art the keeper of my days,
In whom my scattered purposes are stored,
Who turn'st my crooked wandering into ways
And mak'st my silence speak as with a chord:
What was I ere thy company was mine?
A vessel without pilot on the sea,
A lamp that held but would not give its shine,
A word unsaid that no one wished to free.
Now every morning wears thy name as light,
And every dusk is coloured with thy thought,
And all the middle hours that fill the night
Are threaded through with all that thou hast taught:
    I was a text without a margin's gloss,
    And thou the hand that made me speak my loss.
