CCXXV

The bee that sips from every blossom laid
Across the meadow's indiscriminate reach
Doth carry home a honey half decayed
By being gathered from beyond its teach.
But that same bee, directed to one bloom
Of sovereign sweetness none may replicate,
Brings back a thing so fragrant in its fume
As makes all other honey merely late.
So I, who wandered once through many loves
And gathered from each one a thinning store,
Have found at last the garden that behoves
A constant bee to ask of nothing more:
    For thou art that one blossom, sovereign, whole,
    Whose nectar fills and satisfies the soul.
