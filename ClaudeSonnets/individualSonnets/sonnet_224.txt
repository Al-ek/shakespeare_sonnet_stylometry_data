CCXXIV

How many poets, grey with borrowed praise,
Have spent their instruments on lesser things,
And sung through all their compensated days
The worth of patrons wearing paper wings?
Their verses smelled of obligation's ink,
Their music creaked beneath the weight of hire,
And all their metaphors were made to drink
From wells that taste of nothing but desire.
But I have found a subject not for sale,
Whose virtue stands in need of no enlarge,
Whose least perfection makes my verse grow pale
And leaves me debtor who would be in charge:
    For flattery is easy — truth is hard,
    And thou hast made an honest of this bard.
