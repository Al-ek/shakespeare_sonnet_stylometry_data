CLXI

O, let me be the mirror of thy mind,
That I may show thee all thy hidden worth!
For in thy spirit I a kingdom find,
The only paradise upon the earth.
The king who wears a crown of heavy gold,
Is but a captive to a thousand cares,
But I, who in my heart thy image hold,
Am richer than the wealthiest of heirs.
Then let the world pursue its idle gain,
And let the proud man boast of his degree,
I'll count my losses as a holy gain,
So long as I am well-beloved of thee.
For in thy love I find a sweet content,
That makes me bless the life that I have spent.
