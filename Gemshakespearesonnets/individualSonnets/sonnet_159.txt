CLIX

How many hearts have fallen in the snare,
Of painted beauty and a borrowed face!
But thou art made of substance far more rare,
And in thy truth I find a holy grace.
The rose may lose its perfume in the gale,
And lilies wither in the winter's frost,
But thy perfection never shall grow pale,
Nor shall the treasure of thy soul be lost.
For nature carved thee with a steady hand,
And gave thee all the riches of her store,
To be a pattern to a weary land,
And live in memory for evermore.
Let others boast of titles and of gold,
Thy worth is more than can be bought or sold.
